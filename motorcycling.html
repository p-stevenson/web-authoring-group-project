How to get started with Motorcycling?

Motorcycling combines freedom, concentration, and technical skill. A good rider is not just turning the throttle and leaning into corners; they are reading traffic, planning ahead, checking their machine, and making calm decisions in changing road and weather conditions.

For beginners in Ireland, the UK, and across Europe, the best starting point is proper training, the right licence route, and dependable protective gear. A manageable bike, a consistent pre-ride safety check, and regular practice with observation, braking, and slow-speed control will build confidence.

Start Your Journey

Three clear stages can take you from your first training session to confident touring and advanced rider development.

1

BEGINNER

Start with the correct learner route for where you live: learner permit and IBT in Ireland, or a provisional licence and CBT in the UK. Choose CE-rated gear, a sensible first bike, and practice slow control, observation, lifesavers, and emergency stops in low-risk spaces.

2

ENTHUSIAST

Build consistency through regular rides, better road positioning, smoother cornering, and routine maintenance checks. This is the stage to add BikeSafe, an enhanced rider assessment, or refresher coaching so your habits become safer and more deliberate.

3

EXPERT

Advanced riders focus on precision, planning, and long-term judgement. They keep improving through advanced tuition, tour preparation, weather and fatigue management, and often support newer riders with advice, route planning, and strong road discipline.
